A1

Present Simple Tense

The present simple tense is used to describe routines, habits, and general truths. The structure is: Subject + base verb (add 's' for third person singular).

Examples:

  • I eat breakfast every day.
  • She works at a bank.

Subject Pronouns

Subject pronouns are used to indicate who is performing the action of the verb. The subject pronouns are: I, you, he, she, it, we, they.

Examples:

  • I am a student.
  • They are teachers.

Articles (a, an, the)

Articles are used before nouns to define them as specific or unspecific. 'A' and 'an' are indefinite articles, while 'the' is a definite article.

Examples:

  • I saw a cat. (Any cat)
  • The cat is on the roof. (A specific cat)

Basic Questions

Questions are formed using question words (who, what, where, when, why) and auxiliary verbs (do, does, is, are).

Examples:

  • What is your name?
  • Where do you live?

Common Verbs

Basic action words used frequently in everyday conversation.

Examples:

  • Go
  • Eat
  • Sleep
  • Make
  • Do

Basic Nouns

Everyday objects and concepts that are fundamental in basic communication.

Examples:

  • Book
  • Car
  • House
  • Apple
  • Friend

Adjectives for Descriptions

Words used to describe nouns, giving more detail about them.

Examples:

  • Big
  • Small
  • Happy
  • Sad
  • Tall

Useful Phrases

Common expressions used in everyday conversation to help with communication.

Examples:

  • How are you?
  • Nice to meet you

A2

Present Continuous Tense

The present continuous tense is used to describe actions happening at the moment of speaking. The structure is: Subject + am/is/are + verb+ing.

Examples:

  • I am eating lunch now.
  • She is reading a book.

Comparatives and Superlatives

Used to compare two or more things. Comparative: adjective + er or more + adjective. Superlative: adjective + est or most + adjective.

Examples:

  • Taller (comparative), tallest (superlative).
  • More interesting (comparative), most interesting (superlative).

Simple Past Tense

Used to describe actions that happened and finished in the past. Structure: Subject + past verb form.

Examples:

  • I visited Paris last year.
  • They watched a movie yesterday.

Future Forms

Used to talk about actions that will happen in the future. Will: Subject + will + base verb. Going to: Subject + am/is/are + going to + base verb.

Examples:

  • I will call you tomorrow.
  • She is going to travel next month.

B1

Past Continuous Tense

Used to describe actions that were ongoing in the past. Structure: Subject + was/were + verb+ing.

Examples:

  • I was watching TV when she called.
  • They were studying all night.

Present Perfect Tense

Used to describe actions that occurred at an unspecified time and have relevance to the present. Structure: Subject + have/has + past participle.

Examples:

  • I have finished my homework.
  • She has visited the museum.

Conditionals (Type 1)

Used to talk about real and possible situations. Structure: If + present simple, will + base verb.

Examples:

  • If it rains, I will stay home.
  • If you study hard, you will pass the exam.

Comparative Structures

Used to compare two or more items. Structure: Comparative adjective + than.

Examples:

  • This book is more interesting than that one.
  • She is taller than her brother.

B2

Past Perfect Tense

Used to describe actions completed before another point in the past. Structure: Subject + had + past participle.

Examples:

  • I had finished my work before he arrived.
  • She had already left when we called.

Future Perfect Tense

Used to describe actions that will be completed before a certain future time. Structure: Subject + will have + past participle.

Examples:

  • By next year, I will have graduated.
  • She will have completed the project by Monday.

Conditionals (Type 2 and 3)

Type 2: Unlikely or hypothetical situations. Structure: If + past simple, would + base verb.

Type 3: Past situations that didn't happen. Structure: If + past perfect, would have + past participle.

Examples:

  • Type 2: If I were rich, I would travel the world.
  • Type 3: If I had known, I would have come earlier.

Modals of Deduction

Used to make deductions about present and past situations. Structure: Modal verb + base verb.

Examples:

  • Present: She must be at home. (certain)
  • Past: They might have missed the train. (possible)

Idiomatic Expressions

Common expressions with meanings not directly related to the individual words.

Examples:

  • Break the ice
  • Hit the nail on the head
  • Let the cat out of the bag

Formal and Informal Language

Differences between language used in formal and informal contexts.

Examples:

  • Formal: I would like to request your assistance.
  • Informal: Can you help me out?

Advanced Phrasal Verbs

Combinations of verbs and prepositions/adverbs with more complex meanings.

Examples:

  • Give up (quit)
  • Look forward to (anticipate)
  • Run out of (deplete)

Complex Sentences

Sentences with more than one clause, often with subordinating conjunctions.

Examples:

  • Although it was raining, we went for a walk.
  • She will succeed if she works hard.

C1

Mixed Conditionals

Conditionals that mix different times, such as present and past.

Examples:

  • If I had studied harder, I would be in a better job now.
  • If she were more confident, she would have applied for the position.

Complex Sentence Structures

Advanced structures with multiple clauses and advanced punctuation.

Examples:

  • Despite the fact that it was late, she continued working, and her effort was appreciated.
  • Not only did he finish the report, but he also presented it to the board.

Advanced Passive Voice

Used to emphasize the action or the recipient of the action rather than the performer.

Examples:

  • The novel was written by the famous author.
  • The project will be completed by the end of the month.

Subjunctive Mood

Used to express wishes, suggestions, or hypothetical situations.

Examples:

  • I wish she were here.
  • It is important that he be present at the meeting.

Advanced Vocabulary and Collocations

More sophisticated words and phrases that are used in academic and professional settings.

Examples:

  • Meticulous

  • Substantial

Formal Register

Language used in formal contexts, such as academic papers or business communications.

Examples:

  • In conclusion, it can be stated that...

Specialized Terminology

Terms specific to certain fields or professions.

Examples:

  • Algorithm (Technology)

Words and phrases with subtle differences in meaning or usage.

Examples:
